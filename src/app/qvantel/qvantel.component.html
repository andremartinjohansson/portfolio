Background

Qvantel is leading the evolution of Digital BSS. Headquartered in Finland, with over 20 offices world-wide, Qvantel is a global, product-based company. Qvantel delivers software for over 230 million telecoms customers covering mobile, fixed and TV services.

Area

CRM tool for Sales and Care: A powerful and configurable tool-suite for daily sales and customer care with 360 visibility.

Role

Experience Designer. Previously a team, then lone designer embedded with product managers, engineers and developers.

Blurred-out example of Sales & Care CRM.

Sales & Care CRM

Features include:

  • Customer management
  • Customer interaction logging
  • Subscription management for mobile, fixed-line and TV.
  • Device management.
  • Billing, invoicing and payments.
  • Top-ups.
  • Order management.
  • Purchase of new subscriptions and products.
  • And more...

The Qvantel 360 Care Toolbox empowers DSP's front and back-office teams to proactively drive customer interactions and account operations, in such cases where despite focusing on digital self-care, assistance is needed.

Additionally, it provides Operators' Point-of-Sales representatives with all necessary functionalities for customer onboarding, as well as in life cross- and up-sales, across B2C and SMB customer segments.

This is the flagship product and I have had full ownership of the complete experience and UI.

I have lead and driven the product design with focus on effectivness for operators helping customers with their subscriptions and products, whether it's over phone or in store.

Major challenges have included very high stack complexity and many technical limitations making simple design approaches and good user experience much less straight forward.

OOTB Google Analytics app

Analytics

Recently Qvantel started using Google Analytics for collecting user and application data in order to more effectively identify improvement areas, priorities and general usage in the products

The project landed on me to manage the roll-out to different customers and deciding on its collection and presentation in the Google Analytics tool.

More importantly I have been responsible for analyzing the data coming in and presenting my analyses and conclusions to the rest of the company.

UX Management

As lone designer leading the company's flagship product, I've had to take on a bit of a UX manager role. As Qvantel has been innovating, new UX strategies have been needed and I landed in a position where I have needed to define the new UX vision and strategies to support the new ways of doing things.

This includes defining best practices and training material, in order to have a unified UX and CX across the company.

Conclusion

Qvantel's flagship product is a powerful and configurable tool-suite for daily sales and customer care with 360 visibility.

To alone have ownership of such a big and complex product has come with its own unique set of challenges. With the added responsibilities that Google Analytics and UX management brought, Qvantel came to be a great place for learning and growth.
